THIS WEEK IN WHATEVER!

May 2 - May 4, 2025

FRIDAY - THERE WILL BE SEVEN

Rockets force Game 7 vs. Golden State with 115-107 win.
May 2, 2025

In San Francisco, the Houston Rockets and Golden State Warriors played a Game 6 in the first round of the playoffs. With the Warriors leading 3-2, despite the series deficit, there was a clear greater desire to win for Houston, facing elimination. The star of the show for Houston would be Fred VanFleet, with 29 points and 8 assists. Despite a back-and-forth first quarter, Houston would certainly carry the upper-hand, never trailing for the remainder of the game and icing any hopes of a Warriors comeback with a 12-0 run in the fourth quarter. Game 7 will be played in Houston, and is slated for a 7:30 PM start time on Sunday.


SATURDAY - A BATTLE OF COUSINS...

The Kentucky Derby.
May 3, 2025

It is now time for the "Cornholes" in John's Sports Blog--the unique sports we do not often cover here. The Derby is an American institution. The first of the three "Triple Crown" horse races alongside the Preakness Stakes and the Belmont Stakes, the Derby, with its wacky hats and the brief time-frame of the event, lives up to "The Most Exciting Two Minutes in Sports" label. This year, all 19 horses racing had one common trait: they were all descendants from Secretariat, the legendary race horse that won the Triple Crown in 1973, breaking numerous records along the way. Therefore, the Kentucky Derby is now a family reunion for horses. As for the race itself, amidst muddy conditions in Louisville, it would be the horse Sovereignty breaking through down the stretch, fending off Journalism at the end. If you are wondering, Sovereignty and his jockey will not compete for the Triple Crown and will not race in the Preakness Stakes.


SUNDAY - THE MIRACLE IN MANITOBA

Jets pull off miraculous comeback vs. St. Louis in Game 7.
May 4, 2025

Down 2-0 at the end of the first period, 3-1 with two minutes remaining, the 1-seeded Winnipeg Jets have pulled off one of the greatest hockey playoff comebacks in recent memory. With time running out, Winnipeg emptied the net to start a 6 on 5 situation, something the 8-seeded St. Louis Blues have struggled with all season. Vladislav Namestnikov cut the deficit to one with a bounced puck that got past Blues netminder Jordan Binnington. After St. Louis' Pavel Buchnevich empty net shot in the final minute went high, and with time running out, Kyle Connor's desperation shot for Winnipeg went wide, but right into the stick of Cole Perfetti who scored on the open net with 1.6 seconds remaining. After overtime served as a stalemate, the game went into double overtime, where against the gassed Blues defense, Adam Lowry's game winning tip-in was all but a formality to complete the 4-3 comeback win and send Winnipeg to Dallas for the next round.
